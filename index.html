52-Week High Line

Plots the critical 52-week resistance boundary line, tracking price breakouts, percentage distances, and proximity thresholds with customizable styling.

Pine Script v6

Standard 52-week highs are vital benchmarks for institutional traders. This indicator draws a clean level axis extending to the right price scale, avoiding visual noise. Includes a proximity check trigger.

Key Features:
  • Adaptive Lookback: Scales down dynamically if the asset listing has fewer than 252 bars, preventing script errors on newer charts.
  • Distance Tracker: Computes the exact percentage gap to the 52-week high, helping to assess breakouts.
  • Right Axis Plot: Plugs directly into TradingView's scale.

Line Color

Color of the level line on the chart.

Default: Orange

Line Style

Visual texture of the drawn high line.

Options: Solid, Dashed, Dotted

Show Distance %

Toggles showing the percentage proximity label.

Default: True

Proximity Alert (%)

Margin distance threshold triggering warnings.

Default: 2.0%
Alert Name Trigger Event Message Payload
Near 52W High Fires when the asset close price reaches the warning threshold (e.g. within 2%). Price is near the 52-Week High!
Broke 52W High Fires on a cross above the 52-week high level. Price broke above the 52-Week High!

MCDX Smart Money

An RSI-based momentum classifier that segments market participants into Bankers (Smart Money), Hot Money (speculators), and Retailers.

Pine Script v6

Recreation of the popular MCDX indicator. It estimates the volume concentration of different participant tiers, presenting them as colored histogram columns. Dominance shifts trigger background signals.

Composition Levels:
  • 🔴 Bankers (Smart Money): Institutional accumulation. Large columns indicate major support. Very Bullish.
  • 🟡 Hot Money: Short-term speculative velocity and momentum. Transitional state.
  • 🟢 Retailers: Small retail accounts. High retail dominance suggests risk of dumps or tops.

Banker RSI Base

Centerline baseline offset for Banker calculation.

Default: 50

Banker RSI Period

Lookback length for Banker RSI calculation.

Default: 50

Banker Sensitivity

Multiplication scale factor for Banker momentum growth.

Default: 1.5

Show % Labels Table

Displays real-time panel stats in the top-right corner.

Default: True
Alert Name Trigger Event Message Payload
Banker Crossover Hot Money Banker value crosses above Hot Money. MCDX: Banker crossed above Hot Money — Bullish signal 🔴
Banker Crossover Retail Banker value crosses above Retailer. MCDX: Banker crossed above Retailer — Strong Bullish 🔴🔴
Strong Banker Zone (>= 15) Banker value sits at or above 15 of 20. MCDX: Banker in Strong Zone (≥15) — Institutional accumulation 🏦

Pine Script v6

Designed for systematic executions. Overwrites default chart candle coloring based on momentum stages. Features a dynamic dynamic support ribbon (Pillow Support) and institutional overhead resistance (Shark Zone).

ZigZag Swing Wave Indicator

Traces core market structure by filtering out short-term fluctuations, connecting verified pivot highs and lows with percentage changes.

Pine Script v6

Removes market noise to clearly reveal higher highs and lower lows. Connects pivot points with trend-colored lines (bullish/bearish) and provides optional price tags detailing percentage differences.

Key Features:
  • Live Developing Leg: Draws a dotted preview line from the last confirmed pivot point to the current active close price.
  • Directional Coloring: Up-waves are painted emerald, down-waves crimson.
  • Percentage Metrics: Shows structural extension magnitudes directly on chart labels.

Understanding Order Book Spoofing

Spoofing is a manipulative practice where market participants place large orders that they have no intention of executing. These fake walls are set up to pressure retail traders into panic buying or selling, only to be canceled when the price gets close.

Breakouts vs Breakdowns

When the price eats through a heavy ask wall, it triggers momentum buy stops, generating a strong bullish breakout. Conversely, collapsing bid support walls trigger automated sell stops, leading to immediate price breakdowns.

The Bid-Ask Spread Dynamics

The spread represents the gap between the highest buyer bid and lowest seller ask. Low spreads indicate deep market liquidity and heavy trading volume. Wide spreads usually occur during low liquidity or high-volatility news events.
